Professional Development Plan & SMART Goals

I am currently very comfortable working in Unity and C#, and am becoming increasingly familiar with Javascript and web development. I’ve also had some experience with 3D modelling and feel that I am reasonably well acquainted with most aspects of asset design. I feel my real strength lies in the game concepts I’ve produced, both those that I’ve created prototypes of and those that I’m still ruminating on.

In the longest term my goal is to reach the role of lead game designer or producer at a small or medium scale studio (nothing AAA) and I already have a game idea that I think needs to be saved until this time to reach its full potential. Prior to this I would naturally aim to work my way up through the games industry, and while I do have experience with the development and coding side I would prefer to stay on the design side of things if I can.

In the medium term I would like to develop a full, but small, game entirely on my own, to a higher quality than the prototypes I developed during undergrad. I have a few ideas for this which would probably be very achievable. That said, the game I really want to make is a fully realised version of “Trouble’s Brewing”, the VR game I worked on as part of a team in second year. I think that game is the most promising and interesting idea I’ve worked on.

Short term my goal is to continue building on my technical skill set. There are a number of technologies that I feel I need to become better acquainted with before I can put out an entire game on my own. Chief among these is the use of databases, which I only recently started working with. I think I have the basics down but I can definitely get better at implementing them. Ideally I’d like to learn how to use Unreal Engine, as it seems to be a more commonly used tool than Unity in bigger studios and would improve my employability.

I think for the most part completion of this masters course will boost me along the way to achieving my long term goals, however it may further benefit me to get more specific accreditation in some of the key tools of the trade. I am aware that LinkedIn has a system of awarding accreditation to listed skills on completion of tests, but I haven’t been able to determine if those actually have value in the eyes of employers. I should aim to reinforce my portfolio considerably, cutting it down to a few more impressive projects rather than showing my entire history of work. Further to this I should make more effort to push my portfolio out there, and establish a more noticeable online presence generally. I think working on a long term development project would help with this, as it would naturally generate content that I can share as I progress. I’ve seen this used successfully to build online presence and to build hype for indie games before, so I see no reason why it wouldn’t work for me.

By the end of this course I hope to have my short term goals realised so I’m in a position to pursue the longer term ones. While I would like to generate something entirely new for the Devised Project module that might also present an opportunity to begin a new effort on “Trouble’s Brewing”, which I could then continue working on as I enter the workplace. If my efforts to break into the game industry don’t immediately succeed (which is likely given everything that’s going on in the world) I can continue to work on “Trouble’s Brewing” or another project while I work casually, something I’m not actively averse to. That course might also provide me with time to learn Unreal Engine and other valuable technologies.

SMART Goal #1

I want to use a new technology or tool over the course of this module, either as the core development environment for the project or just to improve the quality of the artefact I produce. By the end of the module I want to be at least comfortable with the basics of the tool, but I aim to be confident in my ability to implement it, and in my understanding of its uses.

SMART Goal #2

I feel I have a serious problem with overextending myself on projects, planning too many features and trying to get them all implemented before a deadline. My goal both for this module and going forward is to limit myself to only a few features to begin with. Once these features are complete, properly functioning, and user tested, I will begin to consider additional features, one at a time. I aim to maintain a pattern of controlled iteration.
