Monster Factory is a game about a down on his luck villain who discovers a disused castle. In a bid to make a comeback the player will turn the castle into a fully operational Monster Factory, and create minions of various functions and forms for the other villains of the land.The player will build and upgrade equipment, complete contracts by designing and producing monsters, and raid the area around their castle for materials and funds.

The original inspiration for the game, and its namesake, is the Polygon YouTube video series "Monster Factory", where Griffin and Justin McElroy use the character creators of various games to produce questionable character designs.

This was a team project with 4 members, including myself. As the goal was to present a game with commercial potential we were required to assign ourselves production roles within the team. I took on the role of Lead Developer, and subsequently produced most of the code and technical implementation; as well as the role of Assistant Producer, wherein I had a significant role in designing the mechanics of the game and took responsiblity for keeping everyone else's work on track.

Due to the disruption caused by the COVID-19 pandemic and the communication issues that came with early remote work I feel this project suffered, and is ultimately poorly executed. Despite this unfortunate outcome I believe the core concept of the game has a lot of promise, and under better circumstances probably could have been released in full.

This project's saving grace is the artwork provided by my teammate and friend Ash Kennedy. It really captures the spirit of what we were trying to create and it would be remiss of me not to point you in her direction: ashkennedy.co.uk
