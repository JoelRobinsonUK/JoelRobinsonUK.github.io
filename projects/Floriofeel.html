Visit the App

Floriofeel is a mood tracking app that uses imagery of flowers based on Victorian era floriology (the language and meanings of flowers). Users can log how they feel from a selection of emotions, each represented by a flower. They'll see how many other users have felt the same way recently, and receive a few simple activity suggestions, called nudges.

Aside from a group project in my third year where I handled the front end, Floriofeel was my first time working with Progressive Web App technology. It is by far the most challenging project I've worked on, and I've very pleased with how well it came together.

Among other firsts, this project marks the first time I've worked with databases. It's powered by Google Firebase, and uses both the Firestore database and Authentication functionality of that service. That means that users can sign into their account from any device, mobile or computer, and see their history.

As the app is meant to provide a personal, self-guided experience I would be comfortable saying that the project is complete, as I have personally that it helps me keep track of my feelings. I intend to leave it up, though I likely will not return to work on it, and certainly won't try to market it.
