This game was developed in under 46 hours for the 2020 Global Game Jam. This was the third time I had participated in the Jam while at Bath Spa and the first time that my team had managed to finish a game. My contributions to this game included programming, sound design, level design, and bringing everyone tea.

This playthrough shows the game in its entirety, amazing how 48 hours makes a whole 2 minutes of game play.

MBZ is an atmospheric puzzle game about repairing a run down space station. Due to the time constraint the assets are fairly simple and limited, but the identity of the game is there and could definitely become something more with time.

If you have SteamVR installed when you try to play the game will it boot up. This is because we originally tried to make the game in VR, something that the concept definitely would have benefitted from, but our computer lab had just upgraded to wireless HTC Vive headsets and there were too many technical issues for us to enjoy developing in VR.

This has become one of my favourite projects, despite how simple it seems on the surface, because of how well the team worked together to make it so quickly.

I had the joy of working with Saul Ashbridge (puzzle design, programming), Ash Kennedy (2D assets, programming, animation), and Jack Cotton (3D modelling, UI).
